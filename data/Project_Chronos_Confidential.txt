PROJECT CHRONOS - CONFIDENTIAL BRIEFING
Date: October 24, 2032
Classification: TOP SECRET // EYES ONLY

1. OVERVIEW
Project Chronos is a highly experimental initiative designed to integrate quantum entanglement with neuromorphic processors to achieve zero-latency global communications. The project is spearheaded by Dr. Aris Thorne (Lead Architect) and Dr. Elena Rostova (Quantum Mechanics Director). 

2. THE Q-CORE ARCHITECTURE
The foundation of Project Chronos is the Q-Core (Quantum-Core). Unlike traditional binary systems, the Q-Core utilizes Qubits stabilized by a proprietary synthetic diamond lattice. The optimal operating temperature for the Q-Core is exactly 0.004 Kelvin. If the temperature exceeds 0.009 Kelvin, the entanglement coherence collapses, triggering an automatic failsafe known as "Protocol Icarus".

3. PROTOCOL ICARUS
Protocol Icarus is a self-destruct sequence for data. If the Q-Core breaches the 0.009K threshold, Protocol Icarus executes within 4 milliseconds. It completely wipes the synthetic diamond lattice by flooding the chamber with super-cooled liquid helium, rendering all stored quantum data unrecoverable. It is vital that cooling systems never fail.

4. DEPLOYMENT TIMELINE
- Phase 1 (Alpha Testing): Completed January 2031. Achieved 99.8% entanglement over 14 miles.
- Phase 2 (Beta Integration): Started March 2032. Currently attempting to connect the New York node with the Geneva node.
- Phase 3 (Global Launch): Scheduled for December 2035, pending the resolution of the "Phantom Echo" anomaly.

5. THE PHANTOM ECHO ANOMALY
Dr. Rostova's team discovered that messages sent through the Q-Core sometimes arrive 0.001 seconds before they are sent. This violation of apparent causality is dubbed the "Phantom Echo." While harmless for plain text, this anomaly causes severe desynchronization in real-time video feeds. To counteract this, the engineering team uses a Chrono-Buffer algorithm to artificially delay incoming packets by exactly 2 milliseconds.
